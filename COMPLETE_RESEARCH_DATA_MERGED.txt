--- KEY FINDINGS ---

Finding 1: BART Dominates News Summarization
  CNN/DailyMail BART LoRA/QLoRA: 0.5599 ROUGE-1 - highest across all 45 experiments.
  Encoder-decoder architecture is ideally suited for news summarization tasks.

Finding 2: T5 Excels on Structured and Legal Text
  Best on SAMSum (T5 LoRA: 0.4555) and BillSum (T5 Adapters: 0.4608).
  T5's text-to-text framework generalizes well across diverse domains.

Finding 3: GPT-2 Unsuitable for Dialogue Summarization
  SAMSum GPT-2 scores: 0.0936-0.0979 (near-random).
  Decoder-only architecture without summarization pre-training fails on dialogue.

Finding 4: GPT-2 Achieves Moderate Results with Truncation on Legal Text
  BillSum GPT-2 LoRA (0.2992) and Adapters (0.3019) are reasonable.
  Truncating 1813-token bills to 512 tokens still yields usable summaries.
  QLoRA (E26: 0.2347) slightly lower but still functional.

Finding 5: QLoRA Memory Efficiency Enables Completion on 4GB VRAM
  All QLoRA experiments completed on RTX 2050 (4GB) with batch=1, grad_accum=16.
  Key fixes: gpt2 base (117M) instead of gpt2-medium, label masking for padding.

Finding 6: Adapters Marginally Best Method Overall
  Adapters: 0.3340 avg | LoRA: 0.3295 avg | QLoRA: 0.2725 avg.
  All three methods achieve >99% parameter reduction vs full fine-tuning.
  QLoRA lower average due to truncation constraints on BillSum.

Finding 7: BillSum T5 QLoRA Truncation Sensitivity
  T5 QLoRA (E14: 0.1916) much lower than T5 LoRA (E13: 0.4589).
  T5 is more sensitive to input truncation than BART on long legal documents.
  BART QLoRA (E2: 0.4353) remains competitive despite same truncation.

Finding 8: XSum GPT-2 QLoRA Outperforms LoRA
  E26 QLoRA (0.1551) > E25 LoRA (0.1261) on XSum.
  4-bit quantization with smaller model (117M) can outperform larger LoRA model.

--- METHODOLOGY STATEMENT (for paper) ---

"We conducted 45 experiments across five benchmark datasets (CNN/DailyMail,
PubMed, XSum, SAMSum, BillSum) using three PEFT methods (LoRA, QLoRA, Adapters)
on four transformer architectures (BART, T5, GPT-2, TinyLlama). All experiments
were performed on an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2050 GPU with 4GB VRAM using PyTorch
2.6.0 and CUDA 12.4. Training used 2000 steps with learning rate 2e-4 to 5e-4,
batch size 1-4, and gradient accumulation of 4-16 steps. Energy consumption and
CO2 emissions were tracked using CodeCarbon for all experiments. BillSum and
XSum GPT-2 QLoRA experiments used gpt2 base (117M parameters) with input
truncation to 512 tokens and label masking for padding positions to ensure
compatibility with 4GB VRAM constraints. BillSum GPT-2 experiments (E25, E26,
E27) used input truncation to 512 tokens (from an average of 1,813 tokens),
representing a 72% reduction in input length as a hardware-imposed constraint."
